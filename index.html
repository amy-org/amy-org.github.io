What is Tarot?

Tarot is a deck of 78 beautifully illustrated cards used as a powerful tool for self-reflection, insight, and personal guidance.

It helps you:

  • Understand your current life situation more deeply
  • Explore your subconscious thoughts and emotions
  • Make conscious decisions by seeing hidden patterns
  • Connect with your own inner wisdom

The Rider-Waite-Smith deck (1909) is the most popular because every card has rich symbolic imagery that speaks directly to intuition.

Deck Basics

Structure of the Deck

78 Cards Total

  • Major Arcana (22 cards): Major life lessons and spiritual journey
  • Minor Arcana (56 cards): Everyday situations and practical matters
The Four Suits
  • Wands — Fire: Action, passion, creativity
  • Cups — Water: Emotions, relationships, intuition
  • Swords — Air: Thoughts, truth, challenges
  • Pentacles — Earth: Money, work, physical world

Intuition in Tarot

What is Intuition?

Intuition is your natural inner knowing — a quiet feeling or sense that comes before logical thinking.

Why Intuition Matters in Tarot
  • The beautiful pictures on Rider-Waite-Smith cards are made to awaken your intuition
  • Always look at the card image first and notice what you feel
  • Traditional meanings give structure, but intuition gives personal relevance
  • Intuition helps you apply the card to your unique life situation
Simple Daily Exercise

1. Pull one card
2. Look at the picture quietly for 1 minute
3. Write down the first feelings or story that comes to you
4. Then check the traditional meaning
